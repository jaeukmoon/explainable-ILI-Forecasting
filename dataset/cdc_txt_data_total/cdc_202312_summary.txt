Key Updates for Week 12, ending March 25, 2023:

- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A viruses accounted for the majority of positive specimens, with 67.2% of viruses reported by public health laboratories being influenza A and 32.8% being influenza B.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits were below the national baseline, with 2.3% of visits reported through ILINet being due to respiratory illness (ILI).
- Long-term care facilities reported 0.4% of facilities having at least one influenza-positive test among residents.
- FluSurv-NET reported a cumulative hospitalization rate of 60.8 per 100,000 population.
- NCHS mortality surveillance data showed that 8.3% of deaths in Week 12 were due to pneumonia, influenza, and/or COVID-19 (PIC).
- Four influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during Week 12, bringing the total to 138 for the season.

Overall, influenza activity remains low, with variations across different surveillance components such as virologic surveillance, outpatient illness visits, hospitalizations, and mortality. Influenza A viruses are predominantly circulating, and the majority of hospitalized cases have underlying medical conditions.
