Week28, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with respiratory specimens showing very low positivity for influenza.
2. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during this week; only one pediatric death for the 2020-2021 season has been reported previously.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 1.3% of visits, below the national baseline of 2.6%, with slight increases observed in younger age groups.
4. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 7.1% of deaths, above the epidemic threshold of 5.6%, with COVID-19 as the major contributor.
5. Majority of ILI activity levels across jurisdictions were reported as minimal, with only minor changes noted this week.
6. Healthcare utilization patterns for ILI continue to be affected by alternative care settings due to the COVID-19 pandemic, complicating data interpretation.
7. Virologic surveillance reports limited influenza circulation, with no substantial trends of Influenza A or B positivity currently noted.
8. Hospitalization data for cases after April 30, 2021, are not being updated; historical rates may guide future analyses.
9. The CDC continues to recommend influenza vaccination, but no new vaccination trends were indicated in this report.
10. Future flu activity remains uncertain due to minimal current influenza activity and ongoing COVID-19 dynamics.
