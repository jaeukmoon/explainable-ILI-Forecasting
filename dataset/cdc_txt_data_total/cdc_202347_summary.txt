1. Seasonal influenza activity is increasing in most parts of the country, particularly in the South Central, Southeast, Mountain, and West Coast regions.
2. The predominant influenza virus circulating is Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, followed by Influenza B.
3. Outpatient visits for respiratory illness, including ILI (fever plus cough or sore throat), are above the national baseline.
4. ILI activity is highest in the 5-24 years and 50-64 years age groups.
5. FluSurv-NET hospitalization rates are increasing, with the highest rates among adults aged 65 years and older.
6. The cumulative hospitalization rate for the 2023-2024 season is higher compared to previous seasons.
7. The percentage of deaths attributed to pneumonia and influenza is below the epidemic threshold.
8. There have been no reported pediatric deaths associated with influenza in the 2019-2020 season, but 8 deaths occurred in the 2018-2019 season.
9. Antiviral drugs are available to treat influenza illness, and vaccination is recommended as the best way to protect against influenza and its complications.
