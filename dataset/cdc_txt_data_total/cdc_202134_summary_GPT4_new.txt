Week34, 2021
1. Influenza virus activity is low, with respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza at a rate of 0.1%.
2. ILINet reported 2.2% outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI), below the national baseline of 2.6%, with increases in children aged 0-4 and 5-24.
3. Region 4 surpassed its baseline for ILI, but all other regions are below.
4. Circulating respiratory illness is more likely due to non-influenza viruses, including SARS-CoV-2.
5. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported; only one pediatric death has occurred in the 2020-2021 season.
6. COVID-19 dominated PIC deaths (19.4%), with influenza contributing minimally.
7. Vaccination promotion continues, but vaccination trends were not noted in this report.
8. Outpatient ILI trends and virologic surveillance require cautious interpretation due to altered healthcare-seeking behaviors during the COVID-19 pandemic.
9. Influenza-associated hospitalization data for post-April 2021 is unavailable; updates reflect earlier phases of the season.
10. CDC notes difficulty in predicting flu activity due to simultaneously tracking COVID-19 and other respiratory viruses, compounded by changes in healthcare access.
