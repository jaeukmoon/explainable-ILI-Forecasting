Week50, 2021
1. Influenza activity is increasing nationwide, with 5.6% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza this week.
2. Influenza A virus accounts for 99.2% of positives in clinical labs and 100% in public health labs; subtype H3N2 predominates.
3. Cumulative hospitalization rate for influenza is 1.4 per 100,000, nearly double compared to the previous season.
4. 18.8% of deaths are attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC), surpassing the epidemic threshold, with most due to COVID-19.
5. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) are trending upward at 3.1%, exceeding the national baseline.
6. Eastern and central U.S. report high influenza activity, with western regions showing lower virus circulation.
7. There are early signs that flu vaccination uptake is lower this season compared to last.
8. Two pediatric deaths have been reported this season, both associated with influenza A viruses.
9. Several respiratory viruses, including influenza and SARS-CoV-2, are co-circulating, varying by location.
10. CDC advises flu vaccination remains effective as flu season intensifies; vaccine coverage for influenza A (H3N2) shows some genetic alignment but with antigenic differences.
