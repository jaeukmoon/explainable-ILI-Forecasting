Week52, 2022
1. Influenza activity remains high but is declining in most areas; 15.0% of specimens tested positive this week.
2. Influenza A makes up 99.1% of positive clinical lab results, with 70% being A(H3N2) and 30% A(H1N1). Influenza B accounts for 0.9% of cases.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) were 5.4%, above the national baseline of 2.5%, though decreasing in most regions.
4. The cumulative hospitalization rate is 48.6 per 100,000, 3.5 times higher than typical rates for Week 52 in past flu seasons.
5. 12.8% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19, remaining above the epidemic threshold.
6. A total of 13 pediatric deaths were reported this week, bringing the season's total to 74.
7. Nationwide, all tested influenza viruses remain antigenically similar to those included in the 2022-23 influenza vaccine.
8. CDC reported 22 million illnesses, 230,000 hospitalizations, and 14,000 deaths from influenza so far this season.
9. Flu vaccine effectiveness remains high, and CDC continues to recommend vaccination for individuals aged 6 months and older.
10. Respiratory activity includes co-circulating viruses like SARS-CoV-2 and RSV, impacting ILI data interpretations.
