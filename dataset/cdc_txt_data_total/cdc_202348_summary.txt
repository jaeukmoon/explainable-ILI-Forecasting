According to the CDC's weekly influenza surveillance report for Week 48 ending December 2, 2023, the key facts regarding predicting future ILI occurrence are as follows:

1. Influenza activity continues to increase in most parts of the country, with the southeast and south-central areas reporting the highest levels of activity.

2. Clinical lab data showed 6.8% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza during Week 48, with influenza A(H1N1) viruses predominating.

3. Outpatient respiratory illness surveillance showed that 4.0% of patient visits to healthcare providers were for respiratory illness, above the national baseline and increasing compared to the previous week.

4. FluSurv-NET reported 1,696 laboratory-confirmed influenza-associated hospitalizations so far during the season.

5. Pneumonia and influenza (P&I) mortality was at 0.2% for Week 48, below the epidemic threshold.

6. Four influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during Week 48, bringing the total for the season to 12.

These surveillance data provide insights into the current levels of influenza activity and trends, which can help in predicting future ILI occurrence and guide public health measures.
