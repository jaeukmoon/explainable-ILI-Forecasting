- Seasonal influenza activity continues to decline in most areas of the country.
- The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical labs decreased.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating in public health labs.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses is conducted, and data on antiviral susceptibility are summarized.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits for ILI (fever plus cough or sore throat) remained below the national baseline.
- Geographic spread of influenza varied, with local activity reported in four states and sporadic or no activity in other areas.
- Hospitalization rates due to influenza decreased since January.
- Pneumonia and influenza mortality remained below the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported for the current season.
- CDC recommends annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for flu prevention and treatment.
- The CDC's influenza surveillance system provides comprehensive data to predict and monitor flu activity.
