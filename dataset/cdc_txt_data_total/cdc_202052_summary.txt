Seasonal influenza activity in the United States remains lower than usual for this time of year. The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical laboratories is 0.3%. The number of influenza positives reported by public health labs remains much lower than normal despite a higher-than-normal number of tests performed. ILI activity is at 1.6%, below the national baseline of 2.6%. One jurisdiction experienced moderate ILI activity, two jurisdictions experienced low activity, and the remaining jurisdictions experienced minimal activity. 13.6% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC), above the epidemic threshold of 6.8%. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the week. These figures indicate that flu activity is unusually low at this time but may increase in the coming months. It is recommended to get a flu vaccine and use antiviral drugs to treat flu illness.
