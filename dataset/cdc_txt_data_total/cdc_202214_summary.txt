During week 14, influenza activity increased nationally, with the highest activity observed in the central and south-central regions of the country. Influenza A(H3N2) viruses have been the most frequently detected influenza viruses this season. The percentage of outpatient visits for respiratory illness increased slightly compared to the previous week. Among hospitalized patients, the cumulative hospitalization rate was 8.9 per 100,000 population, which is higher than previous seasons' rates but lower than the rates seen before the COVID-19 pandemic. Three influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during week 14, bringing the total for the season to 19. Mortality surveillance data showed that 7.1% of deaths during the week were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19, above the epidemic threshold.
