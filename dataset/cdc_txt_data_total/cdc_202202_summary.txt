Based on the latest CDC ILI surveillance report:
- Seasonal influenza activity in the United States declined slightly but remains elevated and is expected to continue for several weeks.
- Influenza A(H3N2) viruses have been the most frequently detected influenza viruses this season.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 3.5% of patient visits and remained above the national baseline.
- Long-term care facilities reported 1.0% of facilities with at least one influenza-positive test among residents.
- FluSurv-NET reported a cumulative hospitalization rate of 3.9 per 100,000 population, higher than the previous season, but lower than the seasons before the COVID-19 pandemic.
- NCHS mortality surveillance showed a higher percentage (25.5%) of deaths due to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 compared to the epidemic threshold.
- Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported, bringing the total to five for the season.
- Influenza viruses collected since October 3, 2021, showed genetic similarities with the vaccine virus, with some antigenic differences.
- There's still time to get vaccinated as an annual flu vaccine is the best way to protect against influenza and its complications.

It is important to note that the data are preliminary and subject to change as more reports are received.
