According to the weekly ILI surveillance report from CDC, the key facts regarding predicting future ILI occurrence are as follows:

- Seasonal influenza activity in the United States remains lower than usual for this time of year.
- The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza at clinical laboratories is 0.1%.
- The number of influenza positives reported by public health labs remains unusually low.
- ILI activity remains below the national baseline of 2.6%.
- All jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, but caution should be used in interpreting ILI activity levels due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.
- The current cumulative hospitalization rate for influenza is 0.7 per 100,000 population, which is much lower than average for this point in the season.
- 16.4% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC), which is above the epidemic threshold of 7.2%.
- No new influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the week, and there has been one reported pediatric death for the current season.
- Vaccine effectiveness and antiviral susceptibility data will be available later this season.
- The CDC recommends annual flu vaccination as the best way to protect against influenza and its potential complications.
