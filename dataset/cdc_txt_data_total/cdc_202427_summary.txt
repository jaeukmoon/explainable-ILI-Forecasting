- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating during the reported period.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses and antiviral susceptibility testing are conducted.
- The percentage of visits to healthcare providers for respiratory illness (ILI) remains below the national baseline.
- ILI activity varied by region, with no jurisdictions experiencing moderate, high, or very high activity.
- The hospitalization rate for laboratory-confirmed influenza remains low.
- The percentage of deaths attributed to influenza remains stable and below the epidemic threshold.
- Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the reported period, with a total of 186 pediatric deaths reported for the current season.
- CDC emphasizes the importance of flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for treatment when necessary.
- Comprehensive global influenza surveillance and collaborations with international organizations and countries are in place to monitor the spread and characteristics of influenza viruses.
