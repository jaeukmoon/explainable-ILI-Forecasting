- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating during the reported period.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses are being conducted to monitor changes and compare them to reference viruses used in vaccines.
- Outpatient visits for respiratory illness (ILI) were below the national baseline, with no jurisdictions experiencing moderate, high, or very high activity.
- The hospitalization rate for laboratory-confirmed influenza remained low.
- The percentage of deaths attributed to influenza was stable and below the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported for the current season.
- CDC urges vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for prevention and treatment of influenza.
- External sources such as FluNet, NHSN, and international health organizations provide additional influenza surveillance information.
