Week34, 2023
1. Nationwide influenza activity remains low, with 0.8% of specimens testing positive for influenza in Week 34.
2. Clinical labs reported 63.9% of positive specimens as Influenza A and 36.1% as Influenza B.
3. Public health labs found 52.5% of positive specimens were Influenza A and 47.5% were Influenza B, with all Influenza B classified as Victoria lineage.
4. Hospital admissions for influenza reached 779 patients during Week 34.
5. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 1.7% of visits, with the highest percentage among children aged 0–4 years at 5.8%.
6. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) caused 6.5% of deaths in Week 34, exceeding the epidemic threshold of 5.5%. Nine deaths were attributed to influenza.
7. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported this week; cumulative pediatric deaths for the 2022–2023 season total 172.
8. No novel influenza virus detections, including SARS-CoV-2 variant H3N2v, reported this week.
9. Very high or high ILI activity was limited to specific CBSAs, but the vast majority of jurisdictions observed minimal or low activity.
10. CDC continues to encourage annual influenza vaccination, though no updates on vaccination trends were provided.

11. Summary: Influenza activity remains low overall, with Influenza A dominating among the limited positive cases. Pediatric ILI rates are notably higher, PIC deaths exceed thresholds, and no novel virus detections were reported.
