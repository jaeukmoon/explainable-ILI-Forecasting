Week46, 2023
1. Seasonal influenza activity is increasing in most U.S. regions, with the highest activity observed in the South Central, Southeast, Mountain, and West Coast areas.
2. Of 529 viruses reported by public health labs this week, 79.4% were Influenza A and 20.6% were Influenza B. Among subtyped Influenza A viruses, 79.3% were A(H1N1)pdm09 and 20.7% were A(H3N2).
3. Outpatient respiratory illness accounted for 3.7% of visits, exceeding the national baseline of 2.9% and showing increases in 8 out of 10 HHS regions.
4. Cumulative influenza-associated hospitalization rate was 2.6 per 100,000, the second highest rate for week 46 since 2010, with adults 65+ experiencing the highest rates.
5. Pediatric deaths rose to 3 for the season, with 2 new deaths reported this week, both associated with Influenza B.
6. Influenza-attributed deaths accounted for 0.07% of total deaths, showing no significant change from the previous week.
7. Clinical labs reported 4.9% of specimens testing positive for influenza, a rise compared to the previous week, with Influenza A comprising 66.1% and Influenza B 33.9% of positive cases.
8. CDC estimates 1.2 million illnesses, 12,000 hospitalizations, and 740 deaths from influenza so far this season.
9. Multiple respiratory viruses, including Influenza, RSV, and SARS-CoV-2, are co-circulating, complicating attribution of respiratory illness trends.
10. CDC recommends annual flu vaccination for everyone aged 6 months and older; vaccination trends were not highlighted this week.
