- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- The majority of viruses tested are influenza A, with a smaller proportion of influenza B.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits for ILI (fever plus cough or sore throat) are below the national baseline.
- There is variation in ILI activity across different regions.
- Local influenza activity is reported in some jurisdictions, while others report sporadic or no activity.
- Hospitalization rates and mortality rates from pneumonia and influenza (P&I) are below epidemic thresholds.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season.
- Virologic surveillance provides information on the genetic and antigenic characteristics of circulating influenza viruses and their susceptibility to antiviral medications.
- CDC recommends annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs to prevent and treat flu illness.
- Data is preliminary and may change as more reports are received.
- Additional national and international influenza surveillance information is available through various resources.
