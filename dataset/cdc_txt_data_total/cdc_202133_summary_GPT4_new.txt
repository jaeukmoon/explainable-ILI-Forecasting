Week33, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains low nationwide, with outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounting for 2.2%, below the national baseline of 2.6%.
2. ILI visits have increased for two age groups (0–4 years and 5–24 years) and show a rising trend over the past four weeks across all age groups.
3. Clinical labs report low influenza virus circulation, with increases in ILI more likely caused by other respiratory viruses like SARS-CoV-2.
4. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported for week 33, and only one pediatric death for the entire 2020-2021 season.
5. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 17.2% of deaths, far exceeding the epidemic threshold of 5.4%, with COVID-19 being the primary contributor.
6. No updates on virologic testing or hospitalization rates for influenza as data collection has passed the regular season period.
7. Region 4 reports ILI activity above its region-specific baseline, while all other regions remain below their baselines.
8. High ILI activity is reported in 2 jurisdictions and 27 CBSAs, an increase compared to the previous week.
9. Healthcare-seeking patterns have changed significantly during the COVID-19 pandemic, influencing ILI surveillance interpretations.
10. Low influenza activity continues, with no immediate surge expected, but interferences from other respiratory viruses like COVID-19 could still affect trends.
