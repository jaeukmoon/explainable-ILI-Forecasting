- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- 0.8% of respiratory specimens tested positive for influenza in clinical laboratories.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating in public health labs.
- The percentage of visits for respiratory illness (ILI) remained below the national baseline.
- No jurisdictions experienced moderate, high, or very high ILI activity.
- 0.1 per 100,000 population is the weekly hospitalization rate observed.
- Deaths attributed to influenza remained stable and below the epidemic threshold.
- Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported, one from the current season and one from the previous season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- Annual flu vaccination is recommended for everyone aged 6 months and older.
- Antiviral drugs can be used to treat flu illness.
