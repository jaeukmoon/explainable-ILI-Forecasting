Week49, 2021
1. Influenza activity is increasing, with the eastern and central U.S. seeing the largest rises; western states report lower virus circulation.
2. Positive influenza tests rose to 3.5%, predominantly Influenza A (98.6%), specifically the A(H3N2) subtype (99.8%).
3. Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 2.7% of reported visits, trending above the baseline with increases across all age groups and regions.
4. Influenza hospitalizations are increasing, with 1,057 new admissions this week, though cumulative rates are pending further data.
5. Mortality due to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 reached 17.4% of deaths, primarily driven by COVID-19.
6. Vaccination uptake is lower than last season, but CDC urges vaccination as flu activity begins to rise.
7. Flu-associated long-term care facility outbreaks were minimal, with only 0.5% of facilities reporting at least one positive test.
8. Influenza activity is driven mostly by A(H3N2), with younger individuals (5-24 years) predominant, though infections in adults over 25 are now increasing.
9. Pediatric influenza-associated deaths remain at zero for this season.
10. The flu season is intensifying in some regions, with co-circulation of respiratory viruses complicating ILI assessments.
