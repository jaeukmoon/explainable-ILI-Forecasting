Week22, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with 1.2% of patient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI), below the national baseline of 2.6%.
2. Clinical lab data showed minimal influenza detection, while public health labs reported two influenza cases (one Influenza A, one Influenza B).
3. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 7.5% of deaths, surpassing the epidemic threshold of 6.1%, largely attributed to COVID-19.
4. No pediatric influenza-associated deaths were reported this week; one has been reported for the entire 2020-21 season.
5. ILI visits increased slightly in ages 0â€“4 years but remained stable across all other age groups compared to the previous week.
6. Vaccination data and trends were not highlighted in the report, but CDC continues to recommend annual influenza vaccination.
7. Healthcare-seeking patterns and ILI surveillance remain potentially disrupted due to COVID-19-related behavior changes, with alternative care systems affecting data accuracy.
8. The CDC notes an emphasis on syndromic surveillance combining ILI and COVID-19 data to monitor respiratory illness trends.
9. Geographic spread data collection was suspended for the 2020-21 season, limiting spatial analysis of influenza activity.
10. Future influenza activity remains uncertain, influenced by COVID-19 transmission, healthcare access changes, and low reported influenza levels.
