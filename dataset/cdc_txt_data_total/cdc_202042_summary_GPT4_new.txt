Week42, 2020
1. Influenza activity remains low nationwide, with 0.3% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical labs.
2. Of the 33 positive clinical lab specimens, 54.5% were Influenza A and 45.5% were Influenza B.
3. Public health labs reported 15 positive specimens, all Influenza A (none subtyped) or unspecified Influenza B.
4. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 1.2% of visits, consistent with last week and below the national baseline of 2.6%.
5. No pediatric deaths were reported for the 2020-21 season; two prior-season pediatric deaths were retroactively reported, bringing that season's total to 194.
6. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 7.6% of deaths, surpassing the epidemic threshold of 5.8%.
7. Hospitalization data will be updated later in the season as more reports are collected.
8. Annual flu vaccination is recommended, though current vaccination trends are not detailed.
9. Healthcare-seeking behaviors have shifted due to the COVID-19 pandemic, affecting traditional ILI surveillance and requiring alternate data sources.
10. Flu activity remains uncertain, given current healthcare changes and dual monitoring of influenza and COVID-19.
