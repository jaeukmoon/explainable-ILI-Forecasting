- Seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States.
- Influenza A viruses predominated during the reported week, with equal numbers of H3N2 and H1N1pdm09 viruses reported.
- 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza viruses, which is consistent with the previous week.
- ILI activity remains below the national baseline of 2.4% but increased slightly from the previous week.
- The majority of jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, except for Louisiana which experienced moderate activity.
- Four out of 53 jurisdictions reported local flu activity, while the remainder reported sporadic or no flu activity.
- Hospitalization rates and P&I mortality rates will be updated weekly starting later this season.
- 5% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia and influenza (P&I) during the reported week, which is below the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- Annual flu vaccination is recommended to protect against influenza and its complications.
- Flu antiviral drugs can be used to treat flu illness.
