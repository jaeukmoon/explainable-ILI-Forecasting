Week48, 2021
1. Influenza activity is low but increasing, with 2.6% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical labs, led by Influenza A(H3N2).
2. Influenza A accounted for 97.2% of positive specimens in clinical labs and 99.1% in public health labs, with most Influenza A(H3N2) cases occurring among ages 5-24 but increasing in adults 25+.
3. 2.5% of outpatient visits were for influenza-like illness (ILI), reaching the national baseline, with regions 1, 2, 3, and 7 above their baselines; multiple respiratory viruses are co-circulating.
4. No pediatric deaths due to influenza have been reported so far this season.
5. The proportion of deaths attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) was 17.5%, above the epidemic threshold, largely driven by COVID-19.
6. A total of 841 hospitalizations due to confirmed influenza were reported this week.
7. Flu vaccination uptake shows early signs of being lower compared to the previous season; CDC continues to recommend vaccination for those 6 months and older.
8. Long-term care facilities reported a low rate (0.4%) of at least one positive influenza test among residents.
9. The majority of Influenza A(H3N2) viruses were reported across all 10 HHS regions, with virologic testing continuing and characterization updates pending.
10. ILI visits are trending upwards across all age groups, signaling potential increases in respiratory illness as flu activity begins to rise.
