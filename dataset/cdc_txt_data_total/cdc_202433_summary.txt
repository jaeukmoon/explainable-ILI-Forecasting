- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical laboratories remained stable compared to the previous week.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating in public health labs.
- The percentage of visits for respiratory illness (ILI) reported through ILINet remained stable and was below the national baseline.
- Hospitalization rates remained low, with a weekly rate of 0.1 per 100,000 population.
- 5.0% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia and influenza (P&I), which is below the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported for the current season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- Annual flu vaccination is recommended for everyone 6 months and older.
- Antiviral drugs are available for treatment and should be started as early as possible, especially for higher-risk patients.
