- Seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States.
- 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza viruses, which is consistent with the previous week.
- Influenza A viruses predominated, with equal numbers of H3N2 and H1N1pdm09 viruses reported.
- The percentage of visits to healthcare providers for influenza-like illness (ILI) was 1.4%, below the national baseline of 2.4%.
- Most jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, with Louisiana experiencing moderate activity.
- The majority of jurisdictions reported sporadic or no flu activity, with only 4 out of 53 reporting local flu activity.
- 5.0% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia and influenza, below the epidemic threshold of 5.6%.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- The annual flu vaccine is recommended for everyone 6 months or older.
- Antiviral drugs can be used to treat flu illness.
