Based on the surveillance report from Week 5, the key facts regarding predicting future ILI occurrence are as follows:

- Sporadic influenza activity continues across the country.
- The majority of influenza viruses detected are Influenza A(H3N2).
- Genetic characterization and antiviral susceptibility of the viruses are summarized in the report.
- Nationwide, 1.7% of patient visits reported through ILINet were due to respiratory illness, which is below the national baseline.
- Outpatient respiratory illness activity varied by region, with 4 jurisdictions experiencing moderate activity.
- In long-term care facilities, 0.4% reported at least one influenza positive test among residents.
- Cumulative hospitalization rates are 4.5 per 100,000 population, which is lower than previous seasons.
- 26.3% of deaths in the week were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19, above the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the week.
- Viroligic surveillance indicates that Influenza A(H3N2) viruses have been the most frequently detected this season.
