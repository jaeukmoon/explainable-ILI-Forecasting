Week21, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with 1.1% of outpatient visits attributed to ILI, well below the national baseline of 2.6%.
2. Clinical labs reported a low number of positive specimens for influenza; no clear trend was noted in the circulating virus types.
3. Five novel Influenza A virus cases, including three H1N1v, one H1N2v, and one H3N2v, have been identified this season, all linked to swine exposure and showing no human-to-human transmission.
4. No pediatric deaths due to influenza were reported for the current season, and overall mortality from pneumonia, influenza, and COVID-19 (PIC) was at 8.3%, higher than the epidemic threshold of 6.2%.
5. Public health labs continue to conduct virologic surveillance, but the proportion of Influenza A and B subtypes was not explicitly detailed in this report.
6. No influenza-related hospitalization rates were updated, as data on cases post-April 30, 2021, are not currently included.
7. COVID-19 continues to influence healthcare-seeking behaviors, leading to caution in interpreting ILI data from alternative care settings.
8. Vaccination trends were not highlighted, and the CDC continues to recommend annual influenza vaccination.
9. Geographic spread of influenza is not assessed this season due to reliance on impacted ILI data.
10. Future influenza activity remains difficult to predict due to low current activity and the healthcare system's concurrent focus on COVID-19 surveillance.
