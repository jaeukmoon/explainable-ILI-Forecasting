- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating during the reported period.
- Variations in influenza activity were observed across regions, with no moderate or high activity reported.
- The percentage of visits for respiratory illness (ILI) remained below the national baseline, with stability observed in most age groups.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the current season.
- The percentage of deaths attributed to influenza remained stable below the epidemic threshold.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses is ongoing to monitor changes and vaccine effectiveness.
- Antiviral susceptibility testing showed a low percentage of reduced or highly reduced inhibition to antiviral medications.
- Data collection for hospitalizations and influenza spread was affected by the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, leading to limited information.
- CDC emphasizes the importance of annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for prevention and treatment.
- Additional national and international influenza surveillance information is available through WHO, CDC collaborating centers, and other sources.
