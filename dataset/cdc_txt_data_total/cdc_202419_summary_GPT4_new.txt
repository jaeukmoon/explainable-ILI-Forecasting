Week19, 2024
1. Nationwide influenza activity remains low, with 2.4% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza this week.
2. Of clinical lab positive specimens, 64.5% were Influenza A and 35.5% Influenza B; of public health lab specimens, 60.6% were Influenza A and 39.4% were Influenza B.
3. Influenza A(H3N2) remains the dominant subtype, with 60.5% of Influenza A subtyped specimens identified as H3N2, and 39.5% as A(H1N1)pdm09.
4. Outpatient visits for ILI represented 2.0% of total visits, remaining stable and below the national baseline for the sixth consecutive week.
5. The cumulative hospitalization rate reached 80.6 per 100,000 population, with adults aged 65+ having the highest rate at 220.7 per 100,000.
6. Pediatric mortality increased by 3 deaths this week, reaching a seasonal total of 167, primarily from Influenza A-related cases.
7. Only 0.1% of total deaths were attributed to influenza this week, indicating stable mortality trends.
8. CDC estimates seasonal flu has resulted in 35 million illnesses, 390,000 hospitalizations, and 24,000 deaths so far this season.
9. Co-circulating respiratory viruses, including COVID-19 and RSV, continue to contribute to respiratory illness trends.
10. Vaccination remains encouraged as flu activity continues, with prescription antivirals recommended for high-risk groups.
