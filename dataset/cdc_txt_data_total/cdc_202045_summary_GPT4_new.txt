Week45, 2020
1. Nationwide, influenza activity remains low, with 0.2% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza this week.
2. Among clinical lab positive specimens, 63.3% were Influenza A and 36.7% Influenza B.
3. Public health labs reported 6 positive specimens, with 66.7% being Influenza A and 33.3% Influenza B; subtyping data is minimal.
4. Outpatient ILI visits accounted for 1.5%, a slight increase from the previous week's 1.3%, but still below the national baseline of 2.6%; one region's ILI percentage exceeded its regional baseline.
5. No pediatric influenza-associated deaths have been reported for the current season.
6. Deaths attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) were 8.9%, exceeding the epidemic threshold of 6.1%.
7. Hospitalization data updates and detailed virus characterization are pending for later in the season.
8. Annual influenza vaccinations are recommended, though no significant shifts in vaccination rates are highlighted.
9. Alternative healthcare-seeking behaviors during the COVID-19 pandemic impact the accuracy of ILI surveillance.
10. CDC highlights unpredictable future influenza activity due to COVID-19 and evolving healthcare access patterns.
