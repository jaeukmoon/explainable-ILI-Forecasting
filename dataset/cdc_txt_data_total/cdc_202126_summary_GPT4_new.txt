Week26, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza at very low levels.
2. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported this week; only one pediatric death has been recorded for the entire season.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 1.2% of visits, remaining stable and below the national baseline of 2.6%.
4. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 6.2% of deaths, exceeding the epidemic threshold of 5.7%; no deaths were attributed to influenza.
5. Public health reporting indicates continued dominance of COVID-19 and limited or negligible influenza circulation.
6. Healthcare-seeking behavior changes related to the COVID-19 pandemic influence ILI data accuracy, complicating seasonal influenza trends prediction.
7. ILI activity levels remain at minimal levels across 54 jurisdictions, with no areas reporting moderate or high activity.
8. Virologic testing shows no significant influenza determinant data, as positive specimens remain extremely limited.
9. CDC emphasizes the dynamic relation between influenza and COVID-19 trends in interpretation of surveillance data.
10. Flu activity is expected to remain low, but future outcomes are uncertain due to shifts in healthcare patterns and pandemic challenges.
