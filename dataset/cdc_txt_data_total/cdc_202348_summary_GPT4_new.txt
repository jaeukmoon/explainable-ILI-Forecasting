Week48, 2023
1. Influenza activity is increasing nationwide, with the highest levels in the southeast and south-central regions; 6.8% of clinical respiratory specimens tested positive for influenza this week.
2. Public health labs reported that 79.9% of positive influenza specimens were Influenza A and 20.1% were Influenza B, with A(H1N1)pdm09 as the predominant subtype (74.1% of subtyped Influenza A cases).
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 4.0% of healthcare visits, above the national baseline of 2.9% for the fifth consecutive week; all 10 HHS regions are at or above regional baselines.
4. Influenza-associated hospitalizations are increasing, with a cumulative rate of 5.5 per 100,000 population; rates are highest among adults aged 65+ years (13.6 per 100,000).
5. Influenza-associated pediatric deaths increased by 4 this week, totaling 12 pediatric deaths this seasonâ€”most associated with Influenza A(H1N1).
6. 0.2% of deaths nationwide were attributed to influenza, this rate remains stable but is trending upward.
7. CDC estimates seasonal activity has resulted in 2.6 million illnesses, 26,000 hospitalizations, and 1,600 deaths.
8. Vaccination is encouraged as vaccines are still available, and CDC urges early use of antiviral drugs, particularly for high-risk individuals.
9. Multiple respiratory viruses, including influenza, COVID-19, and RSV, are contributing to elevated respiratory illness activity as reported by ILINet.
10. CDC highlights regional variation in influenza B detection, with combined proportions as high as 37% in regions 4 and 6, indicating potential localized shifts in virus circulation.
