Week25, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with clinical laboratories reporting only a 0.1% positive rate for specimens tested.
2. Public health labs noted minimal influenza activity, with no specific detailed information on subtypes stated for this week.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) were 1.2%, consistent with the previous week and below the national baseline of 2.6%.
4. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported this week; overall, only one pediatric death has been recorded for the 2020-21 season.
5. Deaths attributed to pneumonia, influenza, and COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 6.6%, slightly above the epidemic threshold of 5.8%; deaths were primarily linked to COVID-19 rather than influenza.
6. Hospitalization data for influenza-related cases is unavailable post-April 30, 2021.
7. Vaccination trends were not discussed, though annual influenza vaccination remains highly recommended by the CDC.
8. Healthcare-seeking behaviors and data reporting continue to be influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic and alternative care settings, presenting challenges to data interpretation.
9. Future influenza activity is difficult to predict due to current low activity, limited data, and the ongoing impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.
10. Respiratory illness trends are also shaped by simultaneous monitoring of SARS-CoV-2 alongside influenza-like illnesses.
