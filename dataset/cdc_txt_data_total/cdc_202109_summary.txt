- Seasonal influenza activity in the United States remains lower than usual for this time of year.
- The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza is low, both in clinical laboratories (0.1%) and public health laboratories.
- Influenza virus characterization information will be reported later this season.
- Outpatient illness surveillance indicates that 0.8% of patient visits were for influenza-like illness (ILI), which is below the national baseline of 2.6%.
- ILI activity levels were minimal across all jurisdictions.
- Influenza-associated hospitalizations are lower than average for this point in the season, with a cumulative hospitalization rate of 0.7 per 100,000 population.
- 14.6% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC), which is above the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported during the 2020-2021 season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but may increase in the coming months.
- Annual flu vaccination is recommended for protection against influenza and its complications.
