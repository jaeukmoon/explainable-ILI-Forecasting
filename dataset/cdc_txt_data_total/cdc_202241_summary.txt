1. Seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States, but there has been an early increase in most areas.
2. Influenza A viruses, including H3N2 and H1N1pdm09, are the predominant strains reported.
3. The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical laboratories remains stable compared to the previous week.
4. Outpatient illness due to influenza-like illness (ILI) increased slightly from the previous week but remains below the national baseline of 2.4%.
5. The majority of jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, but Louisiana reported moderate activity.
6. Local flu activity was reported in 4 out of 53 jurisdictions, while the majority reported sporadic or no flu activity.
7. Hospitalization rates and mortality rates attributed to pneumonia and influenza are below the epidemic thresholds.
8. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported so far in the 2019-2020 season.
9. CDC recommends annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs to prevent and treat flu illness.
