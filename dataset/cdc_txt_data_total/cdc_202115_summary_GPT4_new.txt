Week15, 2021
1. Seasonal influenza activity in the U.S. remains unusually low for this time of year, with clinical laboratories reporting only 0.1% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza.
2. Of 12 positive specimens from clinical labs, 41.7% were Influenza A and 58.3% were Influenza B.
3. Public health labs reported 6 positive specimens nationwide, with 66.7% being Influenza A and 33.3% Influenza B.
4. The cumulative hospitalization rate for influenza is 0.8 per 100,000, significantly lower than the low-severity 2011-12 season.
5. Deaths attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) account for 12.4% of all deaths, exceeding the epidemic threshold of 6.8%, driven primarily by COVID-19.
6. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) account for 1.1% of visits, stable from the previous week and below the 2.6% national baseline.
7. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported this week; the total for the season is one.
8. Data interpretation is affected by COVID-19, as healthcare-seeking behaviors and monitoring are altered by the pandemic.
9. Only minimal ILI activity was reported in all jurisdictions during Week 15, with no reported increases in regional flu activity.
10. Flu vaccination continues to be emphasized, but no changes or emerging trends in vaccination rates were noted.
