Week20, 2024
1. Seasonal influenza activity is low nationally, with 2% of clinical lab specimens testing positive; 63.2% were Influenza A and 36.8% were Influenza B.
2. Public health labs reported 64 positive specimens, 76.6% as Influenza A and 23.4% Influenza B; Influenza A(H3N2) made up 60.5% of subtyped A viruses, and A(H1N1)pdm09 accounted for 39.5%.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) remain stable at 2.0%, below the national baseline for the seventh consecutive week.
4. Two pediatric influenza-associated deaths were reported in Week 20, raising the total to 169 this season.
5. The weekly hospitalization rate due to influenza was 0.2 per 100,000 people, significantly lower than the season's peak rate of 9.0 per 100,000.
6. Deaths attributed to influenza slightly decreased to 0.1% this week.
7. One human case of avian influenza A(H5N1) was reported in Michigan, marking the third documented U.S. case.
8. CDC estimates 35 million illnesses, 390,000 hospitalizations, and 25,000 deaths from flu so far this season.
9. Influenza vaccination is still recommended, though vaccination trends were not highlighted in the report.
10. Multiple respiratory viruses, including SARS-CoV-2 and RSV, are co-circulating, contributing variably to respiratory illness trends.

11. Summary highlights: Low flu activity persists; Influenza A prevalence dominates; vaccination and antiviral use are recommended; co-circulating respiratory viruses and occasional novel influenza virus cases warrant robust surveillance.
