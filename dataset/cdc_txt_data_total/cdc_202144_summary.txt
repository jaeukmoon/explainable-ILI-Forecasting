Based on the latest CDC Influenza surveillance report:

- Seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States.
- 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza viruses, which is similar to the previous week.
- Influenza A viruses (H3N2 and H1N1) predominated in public health labs.
- Influenza virus characterization information will be updated later in the season.
- 1.4% of visits to healthcare providers were for influenza-like illness (ILI), activity remains below the national baseline of 2.4%, but slightly increased from the previous week.
- The majority of jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, but Louisiana experienced moderate ILI activity.
- Four out of 53 jurisdictions reported local influenza activity, while the remainder reported sporadic or no flu activity.
- 5.0% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia and influenza (P&I), which is below the epidemic threshold of 5.6%.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported for the current season.
- The CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- The CDC recommends annual flu vaccination for everyone 6 months or older, as it is the best way to protect against influenza. Antiviral drugs are also available for treatment.

Please note that the information provided is based on the specific report mentioned and may change as more data is received. For a comprehensive overview, please refer to the CDC Influenza surveillance system overview page.
