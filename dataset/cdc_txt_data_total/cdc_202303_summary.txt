Based on the Weekly U.S. Influenza Surveillance Report, here are the key facts regarding predicting future ILI occurrence:

1. Seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States.
2. 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza viruses.
3. Influenza A viruses, including H3N2 and H1N1, predominated during the reported period.
4. ILI activity remains below the national baseline of 2.4%, but increased slightly from the previous week.
5. The majority of jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, but Louisiana experienced moderate activity.
6. Four out of 53 jurisdictions reported local flu activity, while the rest reported sporadic or no activity.
7. 5.0% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia and influenza (P&I), below the epidemic threshold.
8. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported for the current season.
9. CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
10. Annual flu vaccination and antiviral drugs are recommended for prevention and treatment.

Note: All data are preliminary and subject to change as more reports are received.
