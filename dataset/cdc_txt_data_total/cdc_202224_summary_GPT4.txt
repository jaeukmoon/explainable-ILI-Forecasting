Week24, 2022
- **Viruses, illness, and deaths**: Influenza activity remains low. 97.3% of clinical lab positives and 95% of public health lab positives were Influenza A, predominantly H3N2. Influenza B accounted for 2.7% of clinical positives and 5% of public health positives. One influenza-associated pediatric death (Influenza A) was reported during week 24, bringing the cumulative total for the season to 30.

- **U.S. virologic surveillance**: 2.5% of specimens tested in clinical labs and 0.9% in public health labs were positive for influenza in week 24. Cumulatively, since October 2021, 4.4% of clinical lab specimens tested positive, with 98.5% Influenza A and 1.5% Influenza B. Influenza A (H3N2) was dominant among subtyped specimens in public health labs (99.9%).

- **Cumulative hospitalization rate**: Hospital admissions related to laboratory-confirmed influenza totaled 1,736 for week 24. Historical data continues to reflect cumulative trends for the 2021-2022 season.

- **Trends of deaths attributed to influenza**: Mortality data for week 24 was unavailable due to a system-wide upgrade; updates will resume for week 25.

- **Percentage of Influenza A and Influenza B**: Influenza A dominated with 97.3% (clinical) and 95% (public health), while Influenza B constituted 2.7% (clinical) and 5% (public health).

- **Novel influenza virus (e.g., COVID-19)**: No novel influenza viruses were reported in week 24. CDC continues tracking COVID-19 separately.

- **Vaccination trends**: No updates or recommendations specific to vaccination were included for this report.

- **Outpatient respiratory illness visits**: 1.9% of outpatient visits in ILINet were for influenza-like illness (ILI) in week 24. Young children (0-4 years) had the highest percentage of visits at 8.8%, followed by 3.1% (5-24 years) and tapering with older age groups.

- **Expectation of flu activity from CDC**: Influenza activity is expected to remain low during the summer.

- **Other key factors**: The majority of jurisdictions and regions reported minimal ILI activity, with only a few reporting low to moderate levels. Long-term care facilities reported at least one influenza-positive resident in 0.3% of reporting centers.
