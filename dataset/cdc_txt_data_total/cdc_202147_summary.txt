Based on the weekly ILI surveillance report from the CDC:

1. Influenza activity remains low in the United States but has shown an increasing trend in recent weeks.

2. The percentage of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical laboratories is 1.5%.

3. Influenza A (H3N2) viruses have predominated, but a small number of influenza B viruses have also been detected.

4. Outpatient visits for respiratory illness, categorized as ILI, account for 2.4% of healthcare visits, which is below the national baseline of 2.5%.

5. Some regions have experienced moderate or high ILI activity, while most regions are below their baselines.

6. Long-term care facilities have reported a low proportion of influenza-positive tests among residents (0.2%).

7. Hospitalization rates for influenza will be updated later in the season.

8. Pneumonia, influenza, and COVID-19 mortality rates are above the epidemic threshold (15.5%).

9. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season.

10. The CDC emphasizes the importance of annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for treatment.
