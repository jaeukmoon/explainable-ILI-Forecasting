- Seasonal influenza activity remains elevated in most parts of the country.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09 viruses are the most frequently reported influenza viruses.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses are conducted to monitor their similarity to reference viruses and their susceptibility to antiviral medications.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits are above the national baseline, with varying activity levels across different regions.
- Four states reported local influenza activity, while others reported sporadic or no activity.
- Hospitalization rates and pneumonia and influenza mortality rates are monitored to assess severe disease impact.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported during the current season.
- CDC recommends annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for treatment of flu illness.
- Surveillance systems provide data on virologic activity, outpatient illness, geographic spread, hospitalizations, mortality, and pediatric deaths.
- CDC's FluView Interactive and international surveillance systems provide additional influenza surveillance information.
