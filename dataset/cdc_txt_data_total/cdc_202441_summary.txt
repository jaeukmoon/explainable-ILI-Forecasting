- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A viruses, including H3N2 and H1N1, are predominant.
- Clinical laboratory testing shows that 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested positive for influenza viruses.
- Outpatient illness surveillance reports that 1.4% of healthcare visits were for influenza-like illness (ILI).
- The majority of jurisdictions reported minimal ILI activity, while Louisiana experienced moderate activity.
- The geographic spread of influenza activity includes local flu activity in four jurisdictions, sporadic activity in others, and no activity in six states.
- Hospitalization rates and P&I mortality rates are below epidemic thresholds.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- Annual flu vaccination is recommended for everyone 6 months and older, ideally by the end of October.
- Antiviral drugs are available for treating influenza illness.
- Virologic surveillance, virus characterization, and outpatient illness surveillance contribute to predicting future ILI occurrences.
