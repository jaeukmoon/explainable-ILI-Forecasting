Week30, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with outpatient visits due to influenza-like illness (ILI) at 1.9%, below the baseline of 2.6% but showing increases for all age groups over the last three weeks.
2. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported in week 30; the sole pediatric death reported this season occurred earlier.
3. Laboratory-confirmed influenza-related hospitalizations are no longer tracked after April 30, 2021, due to the seasonal protocol.
4. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 9.0% of deaths, above the epidemic threshold of 5.5%, but influenza was not listed as a cause of any deaths this week.
5. Public health and clinical laboratory data for influenza are not detailed in this summary, indicating extremely low virologic activity.
6. Nationwide ILI activity maps show most regions reporting minimal activity, with some low and moderate activity at local levels.
7. The increase in outpatient ILI visits suggests higher respiratory illness transmissions, though likely influenced by pathogens other than the flu, including COVID-19.
8. The CDC emphasizes cautious interpretation of ILI data due to changes in healthcare-seeking behaviors during the pandemic.
9. Vaccination trends for influenza this week are not reported, but annual vaccination remains encouraged by the CDC.
10. Future influenza trends remain uncertain, with low flu activity but rising respiratory illnesses, potentially indicating upcoming shifts in ILI patterns.
