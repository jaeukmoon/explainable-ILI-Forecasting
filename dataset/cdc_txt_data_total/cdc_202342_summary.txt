According to the latest surveillance report, seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States, with small increases reported in some areas. The majority of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were negative for influenza, with a positivity rate of 1.7%. Influenza A viruses, including H3N2 and H1N1, predominated during the reported period. ILI activity remains below the national baseline, with 2.5% of outpatient visits attributed to ILI. Louisiana experienced moderate ILI activity, while other jurisdictions reported minimal activity. Geographically, 4 out of 53 jurisdictions reported local flu activity, while the rest reported sporadic or no activity. Severe disease indicators, such as hospitalizations and deaths, remain low. No pediatric deaths associated with influenza have been reported for the current season. CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks and recommends annual flu vaccination for everyone 6 months and older. Antiviral drugs are available for flu treatment.
