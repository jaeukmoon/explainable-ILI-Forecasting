1. Influenza activity is increasing in most of the country.
2. The majority of viruses detected are influenza A(H3N2).
3. Genetic and antigenic characterization and antiviral susceptibility of influenza viruses are being monitored.
4. Outpatient visits for respiratory illness, including ILI, increased slightly but remain below baseline.
5. ILI activity varies by location, with some jurisdictions experiencing moderate or high activity.
6. Influenza hospitalization rates are higher than last season but lower than previous seasons before the COVID-19 pandemic.
7. The percentage of deaths attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 is above the epidemic threshold.
8. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the current season.
9. Influenza surveillance data are available at national, regional, and state levels.
10. FluView Interactive provides customizable visual interpretations of influenza data.
11. Other international organizations, such as WHO, also provide influenza surveillance information.
12. An annual flu vaccine is recommended for everyone aged 6 months and older.
13. Antiviral drugs are available for treating influenza illness.
