The key updates for Week 49, ending December 9, 2023, are as follows:

- Seasonal influenza activity is elevated in most parts of the country, with the southeast, south-central, and west coast areas reporting the highest levels of activity.
- In clinical labs, 10.2% of specimens tested positive for influenza, with influenza A(H1N1) viruses being the most frequently reported.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 4.4% of patient visits, which is above the national baseline for the sixth consecutive week.
- FluSurv-NET reported a total of 2,449 laboratory-confirmed influenza-associated hospitalizations so far in the season.
- The percentage of deaths due to influenza was 0.2%, which remained stable compared to the previous week.
- Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during Week 49, bringing the season total to 14.
- Data on virus characterization and antiviral susceptibility are also available.

These updates provide information on the level of influenza activity, hospitalizations, deaths, and virus characteristics, helping to monitor and predict future ILI occurrence.
