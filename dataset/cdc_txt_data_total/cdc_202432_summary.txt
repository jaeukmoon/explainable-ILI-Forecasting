- Influenza activity remains low in the United States.
- 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza viruses.
- Influenza A viruses, including H3N2 and H1N1pdm09, predominated in public health labs.
- ILI activity remains below the national baseline of 2.4% but increased slightly from the previous week.
- The majority of jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, with Louisiana experiencing moderate activity.
- 4 out of 53 jurisdictions reported local flu activity, while the rest reported sporadic or no flu activity.
- Hospitalizations rates and pneumonia and influenza mortality rates will be updated weekly starting later in the season.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported for the 2019-2020 season.
- CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks.
- Annual flu vaccination is recommended for everyone 6 months or older.
- Flu antiviral drugs are available for treatment of flu illness.
