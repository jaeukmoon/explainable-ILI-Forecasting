Based on the latest CDC Influenza Surveillance Report:

1. Seasonal influenza activity remains low in the United States.
2. 3.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza viruses, similar to the previous week.
3. Influenza A viruses predominated, with equal numbers of H3N2 and H1N1pdm09 viruses reported.
4. Influenza virus characterization information will be updated weekly later in the season.
5. 1.4% of visits to healthcare providers were for influenza-like illness (ILI), below the national baseline of 2.4% but slightly higher than the previous week.
6. Most jurisdictions experienced minimal ILI activity, except for Louisiana which experienced moderate activity.
7. 4 out of 53 jurisdictions reported local flu activity, while the remainder reported sporadic or no flu activity.
8. 5.0% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia and influenza (P&I), below the epidemic threshold of 5.6%.
9. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season, while 138 pediatric deaths were reported in the previous season.
10. CDC expects flu activity to remain low but increase in the coming weeks, and recommends annual flu vaccination for everyone 6 months and older.
