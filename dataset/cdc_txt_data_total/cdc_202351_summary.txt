1. Seasonal influenza activity is currently elevated and increasing in most parts of the country.
2. Influenza A viruses, including H3N2 and H1N1pdm09, are the predominant strains circulating.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) are above the national baseline and have remained elevated for several weeks.
4. Hospitalization rates for influenza-related illness are increasing and at a relatively high level.
5. Pneumonia and influenza (P&I) mortality is below the epidemic threshold, indicating lower rates of severe disease.
6. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season, but 138 were reported in the previous season.
7. The CDC recommends annual influenza vaccination for everyone 6 months and older and the use of antiviral drugs for treatment of influenza illness.
8. Virologic surveillance is conducted to monitor the distribution and characteristics of circulating influenza viruses, including genetic and antigenic analysis.
9. Geographic spread of influenza activity varies across jurisdictions, with some experiencing moderate or high activity.
10. The CDC provides additional influenza surveillance information and resources, including international data and collaborating centers.
