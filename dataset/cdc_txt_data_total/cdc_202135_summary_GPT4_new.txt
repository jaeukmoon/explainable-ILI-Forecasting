Week35, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains minimal nationwide, with outpatient respiratory illness visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounting for 0.9%, below the national baseline of 2.4%.
2. Pneumonia and Influenza (P&I) caused 5.4% of reported deaths, slightly below the epidemic threshold of 5.5%.
3. No new influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during this week, but 188 pediatric deaths from the 2019-2020 season had been reported cumulatively.
4. Public health labs did not report significant influenza subtype data for Week 35, and detailed surveillance continues to monitor future trends.
5. Minimal flu activity was observed across 49 states, the District of Columbia, and New York City, while Puerto Rico reported low activity, and Alaska and the Virgin Islands had insufficient data.
6. No significant trends on influenza hospitalization rates or vaccination activities were provided this week.
7. COVID-19 monitoring continues in parallel, with integrated data impacting ILINet trends and analysis.
8. Weekly data is subject to change as new reports are received, and additional virologic and hospitalization surveillance updates are expected later.
9. Summer-season changes in respiratory virus circulation and ILINet system updates may alter future trends and predictions.
10. CDC emphasizes that flu activity tracking is limited due to the concurrent influence of COVID-19 behaviors and reporting.
