- Seasonal influenza activity is increasing in some areas of the country.
- The first human detection of avian influenza A(H5) in the United States was reported.
- Influenza A(H3N2) viruses have been the most frequently detected influenza viruses this season.
- The percentage of visits for respiratory illness (ILI) remains below baseline but has been trending upward in all age groups since February.
- The hospitalization rate for influenza-related illness is higher than the previous season but lower than in seasons preceding the COVID-19 pandemic.
- For pediatric deaths, one influenza-associated death was reported during the current season.
- The majority of deaths due to pneumonia, influenza, and COVID-19 were primarily attributed to COVID-19 rather than influenza.
- The genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses is ongoing, and some antigenic differences have been observed compared to the vaccine reference viruses.
- Influenza viruses tested for antiviral susceptibility show reduced susceptibility to baloxavir in one A(H3N2) virus.
- Additional surveillance information is available through FluView Interactive, state and local influenza surveillance, and international sources.
