Week27, 2023
1. Influenza activity is low nationally, with 0.8% of specimens testing positive for influenza in clinical labs this week.
2. Among positive clinical specimens, 58.3% were Influenza A and 41.7% were Influenza B.
3. Public health labs confirmed 44 positive specimens, with 56.8% as Influenza A (mostly (H1N1)pdm09) and 43.2% as Influenza B (all Victoria lineage).
4. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 1.3% of all visits, reflecting minimal activity.
5. Hospitalizations reported 641 confirmed influenza cases this week.
6. Total deaths from pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) were at 5.5%, below the epidemic threshold of 5.8%; influenza contributed to 5 deaths.
7. Two pediatric deaths were newly reported this week, bringing the season total to 162.
8. Influenza A variants remain more dominant than B, with (H1N1)pdm09 as the main subtype characterized in public health labs.
