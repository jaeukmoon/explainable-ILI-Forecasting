1. Seasonal influenza activity remains elevated nationally with increases in some parts of the country.
2. Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09 viruses were the most frequently reported influenza viruses during the reported week.
3. Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 4.5% of patient visits, above the baseline level.
4. Moderate to high ILI (influenza-like illness) activity was reported in multiple jurisdictions, with 11 jurisdictions experiencing moderate activity and 27 jurisdictions experiencing high or very high activity.
5. Hospitalization rates and mortality rates from pneumonia and influenza remain below epidemic thresholds.
6. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported during the current season.
7. CDC expects flu activity to remain elevated but potentially decrease in the coming weeks.
8. Annual flu vaccination is recommended for everyone 6 months and older, along with the use of antiviral drugs for treatment.
9. Virologic surveillance provides information on the percentage of positive influenza specimens and the distribution of virus types/subtypes.
10. Virus characterization and antiviral susceptibility testing are ongoing to monitor the effectiveness of vaccines and antiviral medications.
11. ILI activity, hospitalizations, and mortality rates should be evaluated in conjunction with other respiratory disease activity, including COVID-19 and RSV.
12. The CDC provides additional national and international influenza surveillance information through various resources and collaborations.
