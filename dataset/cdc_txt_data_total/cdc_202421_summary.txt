- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating during the reported period.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses and assessment of antiviral susceptibility are ongoing.
- 1.9% of visits to a healthcare provider were for respiratory illness, which is below the national baseline.
- ILI activity varied across jurisdictions, with no jurisdictions experiencing moderate, high, or very high activity during the reported period.
- The FluSurv-NET hospitalization rate was 0.2 per 100,000 population during the reported week.
- The percentage of deaths attributed to influenza remained stable and was below the epidemic threshold.
- Three influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during the season, bringing the total to 172 deaths.
- CDC recommends annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for treatment of flu illness.
- Data are preliminary and subject to change as more reports are received.
