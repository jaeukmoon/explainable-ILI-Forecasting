- Seasonal influenza activity is currently elevated and increasing in most parts of the country.
- The majority of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza, with Influenza A(H1N1) being the most frequently reported virus.
- Outpatient visits for respiratory illness, including influenza-like illness (ILI), are above the national baseline and have been increasing in all regions.
- Influenza-associated hospitalizations are increasing, and the hospitalization rate is higher compared to previous seasons.
- The percentage of deaths attributed to pneumonia and influenza (P&I) is below the epidemic threshold.
- No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported during the current season.
- Antiviral medications are available for the treatment of influenza.
- CDC recommends vaccination against influenza for everyone aged 6 months and older.
- Influenza surveillance data from international sources, including the World Health Organization and Canada, are available for further information.
