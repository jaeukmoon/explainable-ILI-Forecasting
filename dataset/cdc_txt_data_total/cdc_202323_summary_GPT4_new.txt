Week23, 2023
1. Influenza activity decreased, with 1.0% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza this week.
2. Clinical labs reported 255 positive specimens, with 54.5% being Influenza A and 45.5% Influenza B.
3. Public health labs detected 39 positive specimens: 33.3% Influenza A (predominantly H1N1 at 90.0%) and 66.7% Influenza B (100% Victoria lineage).
4. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 1.6% of visits, remaining below the national baseline.
5. Hospitalization numbers were at 850 for laboratory-confirmed influenza for week 23.
6. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) caused 6.3% of deaths this week, exceeding the epidemic threshold of 6.1%; influenza accounted for 10 deaths this week.
7. Three influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported, two caused by Influenza B and one by Influenza A.
8. Nationwide, 51 states/jurisdictions reported "minimal" ILI activity, with a slight decline in higher activity levels in CBSAs.
9. No updates or significant trends in influenza vaccination were noted in this report.
10. Ongoing coexistence of other respiratory viruses may affect future influenza trends, with regional variances in influenza contributions to ILI.
