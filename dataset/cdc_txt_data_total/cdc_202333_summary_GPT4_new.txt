Week33, 2023
1. Influenza activity is low, with only 0.5% of respiratory specimens testing positive; 85.5% were Influenza A and 14.5% Influenza B in clinical labs.
2. Public health labs reported 39 positives; 94.9% were Influenza A (96.6% H3N2) and 5.1% Influenza B, with Victoria lineage detected.
3. A novel swine-origin Influenza A(H1N2)v case was reported in a child in Ohio, with no person-to-person transmission identified.
4. Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 1.5% nationally, with 6.3% in children aged 0-4 years, indicating regional variations in ILI contributions from multiple viruses.
5. 10.0% of deaths were due to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC), exceeding the epidemic threshold; 7 deaths listed influenza, while most were COVID-19-related.
6. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported this week; cumulative pediatric deaths for the season remain at 33.
7. 31 long-term care facilities (0.2%) reported at least one influenza-positive resident.
8. 477 patients were hospitalized with laboratory-confirmed influenza this week, reflecting ongoing low hospitalization rates.
9. Data on vaccination trends for this period were not reported or emphasized, while CDC continues promoting annual flu vaccinations.
10. Increased co-circulation of respiratory viruses and altered healthcare use due to the COVID-19 pandemic affect data interpretation and predictions for future ILI patterns.
