- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- Influenza A(H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating during the reported period.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization and antiviral susceptibility of influenza viruses are ongoing.
- The percentage of visits for respiratory illness (ILI) reported through the U.S. Outpatient Influenza-like Illness Surveillance Network (ILINet) increased slightly compared to the previous week but remained below the national baseline.
- No jurisdictions experienced moderate, high, or very high ILI activity during the reported period.
- There were no new human infections with novel influenza A viruses reported.
- The percentage of deaths attributed to influenza remained stable and below the epidemic threshold.
- One influenza-associated pediatric death was reported during the reported period, with a total of 195 pediatric deaths reported for the season.
- CDC estimates that there have been at least 35 million illnesses, 400,000 hospitalizations, and 25,000 deaths from flu so far this season.
- Flu vaccination and antiviral drugs are recommended for prevention and treatment.
