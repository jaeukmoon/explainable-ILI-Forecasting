The key updates for Week 15, ending April 15, 2023, are as follows:

- Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
- In clinical laboratories, 1.0% of respiratory specimens tested positive for influenza.
- In public health laboratories, influenza A(H1N1) and influenza B viruses were the most frequently reported.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses is ongoing.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 2.0% of patient visits, below the national baseline.
- Four jurisdictions experienced moderate outpatient respiratory illness activity.
- In long-term care facilities, 0.4% of facilities reported at least one influenza-positive test among residents.
- FluSurv-NET reported a cumulative hospitalization rate of 61.5 per 100,000 population.
- HHS Protect reported 1,072 patients admitted to hospitals with laboratory-confirmed influenza.
- NCHS mortality surveillance indicated that 7.5% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19.
- Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported, bringing the total to 143 for the season.

These updates provide information on influenza activity, hospitalizations, mortality, and surveillance methods, contributing to predictions and monitoring of future ILI occurrences.
