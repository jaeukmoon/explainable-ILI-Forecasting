Week12, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains unusually low, with only 0.1% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza.
2. Clinical labs reported 20 positive specimens, with 45% being Influenza A and 55% Influenza B.
3. Public health labs identified just one Influenza A specimen, with no subtype characterization; no Influenza B was detected.
4. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) remain very low at 0.9%, well below the national baseline of 2.6%.
5. ILI activity across all U.S. jurisdictions remains minimal, with stable trends across most age groups; only the 0–4 age group showed a slight increase.
6. Cumulative hospitalization rates are extremely low at 0.7 per 100,000, one-seventh of the rate seen during the low-severity 2011–12 season.
7. 11.4% of deaths were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC), primarily driven by COVID-19, exceeding the epidemic threshold of 7.0%.
8. No pediatric deaths were reported for the week; one pediatric death has been reported this season.
9. Vaccine remains the primary preventive measure for influenza, though no updates on vaccination trends were provided.
10. CDC warns flu activity could increase in the coming months, impacted by overlapping COVID-19 trends and healthcare-seeking behavior changes during the pandemic.
