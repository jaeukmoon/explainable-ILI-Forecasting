The key updates from the CDC's weekly influenza surveillance report for Week 20, ending May 20, 2023, are as follows:

1. Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
2. Outpatient respiratory illness visits remain below the national baseline, with 2.0% of patient visits reported through ILINet being due to respiratory illness (ILI).
3. Long-term care facilities report a low percentage (0.3%) of facilities with at least one influenza-positive test among residents.
4. FluSurv-NET hospitalization data indicates a low rate of hospitalization for laboratory-confirmed influenza.
5. NCHS mortality surveillance shows that 6.5% of deaths in Week 20 were due to pneumonia, influenza, and/or COVID-19.
6. Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during Week 20, bringing the total number of pediatric deaths for the season to 154.
7. Virologic surveillance data indicates that 1.1% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical laboratories were positive for influenza, with influenza B viruses being the most frequently reported.
8. Virus characterization analysis shows that the majority of influenza viruses tested are similar to the strains included in the current influenza vaccine.
9. Antiviral susceptibility testing indicates that the circulating influenza viruses remain susceptible to antiviral medications.
10. ILI activity levels vary across states and CBSAs, with some areas experiencing very high or high ILI activity levels, while others have minimal or no activity.

Please note that all data are preliminary and may change as more reports are received. It is important to consult the CDC's weekly influenza surveillance reports for the most up-to-date information.
