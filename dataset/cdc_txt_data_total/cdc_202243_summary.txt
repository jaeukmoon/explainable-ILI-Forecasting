During week 43 ending on October 29, 2022, early increases in seasonal influenza activity continued nationwide, with the southeastern and south-central areas of the country reporting the highest levels of activity. The majority of viruses detected so far this season have been influenza A(H3N2), although the proportion of influenza A(H1N1) viruses is increasing nationally. Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 4.3% of patient visits reported through the U.S. Outpatient Influenza-like Illness Surveillance Network (ILINet), which is above the national baseline, with regions 1-9 reporting activity above their respective baselines. Additionally, influenza-associated hospitalizations and mortality rates were reported, along with data on virus characterization, antiviral susceptibility, and pediatric deaths.
