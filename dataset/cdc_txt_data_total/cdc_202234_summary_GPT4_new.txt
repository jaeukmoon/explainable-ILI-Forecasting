Week34, 2022
1. Influenza activity remains low nationally, with 0.5% of specimens testing positive for influenza during Week 34.
2. Clinical laboratories reported 87.1% of positive cases as Influenza A and 12.9% as Influenza B cumulatively.
3. Public health laboratories also reported 97.5% of positive cases as Influenza A (primarily H3N2), with only 2.5% as Influenza B.
4. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) were 1.8% nationally, remaining below baseline levels, with age groups 0-4 years experiencing the highest percentage (7.0%).
5. One influenza-associated pediatric death was reported, totaling 34 for the 2021-2022 season.
6. Pneumonia, influenza, and COVID-19 (PIC) accounted for 9.2% of all deaths, with influenza contributing to eight deaths, dominated by COVID-19 mortality.
7. Virologic and hospitalization data show limited influenza activity, with 486 influenza-related hospitalizations reported during Week 34.
8. 0.2% of long-term care facilities reported at least one influenza-positive test, indicating minimal spread in vulnerable populations.
9. The CDC did not report updates on vaccination trends this week but continues to recommend annual flu vaccination.
10. Future flu activity remains uncertain as healthcare-seeking behaviors and co-circulating respiratory viruses (e.g., COVID-19) complicate traditional surveillance insights.
