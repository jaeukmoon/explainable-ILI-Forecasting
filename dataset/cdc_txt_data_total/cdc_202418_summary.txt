- Seasonal influenza activity continues to decline in most areas of the country.
- Influenza A (H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses were all co-circulating during the reported period.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses is ongoing to monitor their similarity to the reference viruses used in vaccines.
- Outpatient respiratory illness visits accounted for 2.1% of healthcare visits, remaining below the national baseline.
- Geographic spread of influenza activity varied, with no jurisdictions experiencing moderate, high, or very high activity during the reported period.
- The FluSurv-NET hospitalization rate was 0.3 per 100,000 population, with a cumulative rate of 80.3 per 100,000 population.
- 0.2% of deaths were attributed to influenza, below the epidemic threshold.
- Seven influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported, with a total of 164 pediatric deaths for the current season.
- CDC recommends annual flu vaccination and the use of antiviral drugs for treatment.
- The CDC provides additional national and international influenza surveillance information through various resources and collaborations.
