Week13, 2021
1. Influenza activity remains unusually low nationwide, with only 0.1% of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza.
2. Among clinical specimens, Influenza A accounted for 60% and Influenza B for 40%; public health specimens showed 80% Influenza A and 20% Influenza B, with no specific subtypes widely characterized.
3. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) were stable at 1.0%, well below the national baseline of 2.6%.
4. The cumulative hospitalization rate for influenza was 0.7 per 100,000 population, significantly lower than in previous low-severity seasons.
5. Deaths attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) were 11.4%, above the epidemic threshold, with most deaths attributed to COVID-19 rather than influenza.
6. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported this week; the season total remains at one.
7. Vaccination coverage trends were not specified, but annual influenza vaccination continues to be recommended by the CDC.
8. Healthcare behavior changes due to the COVID-19 pandemic impact ILI and surveillance data interpretation, emphasizing cautious evaluation.
9. All jurisdictions reported minimal ILI activity, with no regions experiencing high or moderate activity levels.
10. Future flu activity is expected to potentially increase in coming months, though prediction remains uncertain due to low activity and pandemic-related changes in healthcare behaviors.
