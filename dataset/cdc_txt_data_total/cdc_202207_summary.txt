1. Influenza activity remains sporadic across the United States.
2. Majority of the detected viruses are Influenza A(H3N2).
3. The percentage of outpatient visits for respiratory illness, including ILI, remains below baseline.
4. Hospitalization rates for influenza are higher than the previous season but lower than previous seasons before the COVID-19 pandemic.
5. The mortality rate attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 is above the epidemic threshold.
6. Only one influenza-associated pediatric death has been reported this season.
7. The majority of circulating influenza viruses are susceptible to antiviral medications.
8. Influenza activity varies by region and age group.
9. Data collected from long-term care facilities show a low percentage of influenza-positive tests among residents.
10. CDC recommends annual flu vaccination for everyone aged 6 months and older to protect against influenza and its complications.
