According to the CDC's surveillance report for Week 46, ending November 19, 2022, seasonal influenza activity is elevated across the country. Key facts include:

- Influenza A viruses, specifically A(H3N2), are the most frequently reported viruses.
- Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses are ongoing.
- Outpatient visits for respiratory illness (ILI) are above the national baseline of 2.5%, with all 10 HHS regions reporting higher than baseline activity.
- Long-term care facilities report 1.6% of facilities having at least one influenza-positive test among residents.
- FluSurv-NET reports 3,312 laboratory-confirmed influenza-associated hospitalizations.
- 9.4% of deaths during the week ending November 19 were attributed to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19, above the epidemic threshold.
- Five influenza-associated pediatric deaths occurred during week 46, bringing the total for the season to 12.
- National and international influenza surveillance information is available through various sources, including FluView Interactive and WHO Collaborating Centers.

These key facts provide an overview of the current influenza situation and highlight the importance of vaccination and surveillance in managing the flu.
