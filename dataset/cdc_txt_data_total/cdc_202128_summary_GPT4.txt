Week28, 2021
- **Viruses, Illness, and Deaths**: Influenza activity is low, with only one influenza-associated death reported during the 2020-2021 season. 7.1% of deaths this week (1,263 total) were due to pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19, with most attributed to COVID-19.  
- **U.S. Virologic Surveillance**: Data on virologic activity is not explicitly provided for week 28.  
- **Cumulative Hospitalization Rate**: No influenza-related hospitalizations are tracked beyond April 30, 2021.  
- **Trends of Deaths Attributed to Influenza**: Influenza-related deaths are currently minimal, with mortality primarily driven by COVID-19.  
- **Percentage of Influenza A and B**: Not provided or analyzed for week 28 in the report.  
- **Novel Influenza Virus (COVID-19)**: Mortality remains primarily driven by COVID-19, with 498 of the 1,263 PIC deaths listing COVID-19 as a cause.  
- **Vaccination Trends**: Not mentioned in this week's report.  
- **Outpatient Respiratory Illness Visits**: 1.3% of outpatient visits were for influenza-like illness (ILI), below the 2.6% national baseline. Slight increases were noted in ILI visits for children (0–4 years), young adults (5–24 years), and adults (25–49 years).  
- **Expectation of Flu Activity from CDC**: Influenza activity is low; no indications suggest a sharp increase in flu activity in the immediate future.  
- **Other Key Factors**: Most jurisdictions report minimal ILI activity, with no states or regions reporting moderate or higher levels. Data caution is advised due to changes in health-seeking behaviors and the overlap with COVID-19 symptoms.
