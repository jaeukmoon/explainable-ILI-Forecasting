Week37, 2022
1. Nationwide, 0.8% of respiratory specimens tested in clinical labs were positive for influenza, with 93.4% Influenza A and 6.6% Influenza B.
2. Public health labs reported 35 positive specimens, with 88.6% Influenza A (90.9% H3N2) and 11.4% Influenza B.
3. A novel influenza A(H1N2)v infection was reported in Georgia, linked to swine exposure, with no person-to-person transmission detected.
4. Outpatient visits for influenza-like illness (ILI) accounted for 2.0% of visits, varying by age group, with the highest among 0-4 years (8.1%).
5. Flu-related hospitalizations totaled 637 during the week, emphasizing continued monitoring of severe cases.
6. Pneumonia, influenza, or COVID-19 (PIC) caused 9.2% of deaths, exceeding the epidemic threshold, though most were due to COVID-19.
7. Two influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported for the 2021-2022 season, bringing the total to 37.
8. Influenza activity remained minimal in the majority of jurisdictions, though some areas reported moderate or higher activity.
9. Continuing co-circulation of multiple respiratory viruses, including COVID-19 and RSV, complicates ILI attribution and surveillance.
10. No updates or significant trends in vaccination coverage were reported in this summary.
