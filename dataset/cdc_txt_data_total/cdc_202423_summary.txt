1. Seasonal influenza activity remains low nationally.
2. Influenza A (H1N1)pdm09, A(H3N2), and B viruses are all co-circulating.
3. Genetic and antigenic characterization of influenza viruses is conducted to monitor changes and assess vaccine effectiveness.
4. Outpatient visits for respiratory illness, including ILI, remain below the national baseline.
5. Geographic spread of influenza varies, with some areas experiencing moderate activity.
6. Hospitalization rates for influenza are currently low.
7. Pneumonia and influenza mortality rates are below the epidemic threshold.
8. No influenza-associated pediatric deaths have been reported in the current season.
9. Recommendations include getting an annual flu vaccine and using antiviral drugs for treatment when necessary.
10. The CDC provides additional influenza surveillance information through various national and international sources.
