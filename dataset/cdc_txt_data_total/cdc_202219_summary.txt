During week 19, seasonal inﬂuenza activity was increasing in parts of the United States. The majority of inﬂuenza viruses detected were Inﬂuenza A(H3N2). The percentage of patient visits for respiratory illness remained stable compared to the previous week but has been trending upward since early March. Among hospitalized adults, the most common underlying medical conditions were hypertension, cardiovascular disease, metabolic disorder, and obesity. The overall cumulative hospitalization rate was 14.4 per 100,000 population. 7.5% of deaths during week 19 were attributed to pneumonia, inﬂuenza, and/or COVID-19, above the epidemic threshold. No inﬂuenza-associated pediatric deaths were reported during week 19.
