- Flu vaccination is especially important for people 65 years and older due to their higher risk of developing serious flu complications.
- During most flu seasons, people 65 years and older bear the greatest burden of severe flu disease.
- Between 70% and 85% of seasonal flu-related deaths occur in people 65 years and older, and between 50% and 70% of seasonal flu-related hospitalizations occur in this age group.
- CDC and the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) recommend the use of higher dose flu vaccines or adjuvanted inactivated flu vaccines for people 65 years and older, as they may be more effective in this age group.
- Prompt flu antiviral treatment is recommended for people 65 years and older who have flu or suspected flu and are at higher risk of serious flu complications.
