- Long-term symptoms of the 1918 flu pandemic, such as loss of muscular energy and nervous complications, bear resemblance to the long-term symptoms of Long Covid experienced by some patients infected with the SARS-CoV-2 virus.
- Long Covid refers to symptoms that persist for several weeks or months after the onset of initial Covid-19 symptoms, whether or not the individual was tested for the virus.
- Research into Long Covid is ongoing, but there are still many unanswered questions regarding the proportion of individuals affected and the range of symptoms.
- Long Covid can include symptoms such as profound fatigue, breathlessness, aches, palpitations, rashes, and pins and needles.
- The flu virus, much like the SARS-CoV-2 virus, can cause damage beyond the respiratory tract, affecting various tissues and organs.
- Studies have been conducted on the long-term effects of the flu virus, providing insights into the potential long-term effects of Covid-19.
