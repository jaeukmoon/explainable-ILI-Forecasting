1. The first human cases of Influenza A(H5N1) (Bird Flu) virus infections in Cambodia have been reported in 2024.
2. The CDC provides information on avian influenza and its prevention.
3. Health care providers have specific case definitions and guidance for investigating human infections with avian influenza A viruses.
4. Antiviral medications can be used for the treatment and chemoprophylaxis of human infections with novel influenza A viruses associated with severe disease.
5. There are infection control guidance and considerations for veterinarians in handling cats potentially exposed to highly pathogenic avian influenza A(H5N1) virus in cats.
6. The CDC also provides public health strategies and guidance for specimen collection and testing for suspected infections with novel influenza A viruses.
7. Close contacts of persons infected with novel influenza A viruses should be followed up, and antiviral medications can be used for chemoprophylaxis.
8. The CDC provides technical reports and summaries on highly pathogenic avian influenza.
9. Bird flu is a separate topic from seasonal flu and swine flu, and the CDC provides information on each of these topics.
10. The current situation and monitoring of bird flu are ongoing, and prevention measures for backyard flock owners are available.
