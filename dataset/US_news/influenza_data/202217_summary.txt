- A person in the US has tested positive for avian influenza A(H5) virus (H5 bird flu) after direct exposure to poultry and involvement in culling poultry.
- The US Department of Agriculture detected highly pathogenic avian influenza A(H5N1) virus in wild birds, commercial poultry, and backyard bird flocks.
- The human health risk assessment for H5 bird flu remains low for the general public, but individuals with work or recreational exposure to infected birds are at a greater risk.
- The CDC recommends patient investigation and testing, infection control measures, and antiviral treatment and prophylaxis in response to potential human infection with H5 bird flu.
- The case in Colorado involves an individual who developed fatigue following exposure to presumptive H5N1 virus-infected poultry; the individual wore recommended personal protective equipment.
- The individual tested positive for influenza A(H5) virus and is currently asymptomatic and in isolation on oseltamivir treatment. The source of the infection is unclear.
