Vaccine Recommendations: The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) provide recommendations for influenza prevention and treatment in children for the 2023-2024 season. Annual influenza vaccination is essential in preventing severe illness and complications, including death, in children.
Clinician Role: Clinicians have a critical role in ensuring the immunization of children and their family members and caregivers. They play an important part in promoting and administering the flu vaccine to protect children and prevent the spread of influenza.
Influenza Threat to Children: Influenza poses a significant threat to children, and vaccination is the most effective way to prevent illness and associated complications. Severe morbidity and mortality can result from influenza infection in children.
Call Details: A webinar titled "2023-2024 Recommendations for Influenza Prevention and Treatment in Children: An Update for Pediatric Providers" was held on Thursday, August 31, 2023. The presenters included pediatrician Dr. Fatimah Dawood from the CDC and Dr. Kristina A. Bryant from the AAP.
Call Materials: Slides and a transcript from the webinar are available for reference.
COCA Call Objectives: The goals of the COCA Call were to provide an overview of influenza prevention and treatment recommendations for the upcoming season and to highlight the role of clinicians in promoting vaccination and preventing influenza in children.
