1. National Influenza Vaccination Week (NIVW) is an annual observance to remind people to get their flu vaccine.
2. Getting a flu vaccine can prevent flu illness, reduce the severity of flu symptoms, and protect high-risk individuals from complications.
3. Flu activity is currently low but may increase during the winter months.
4. Vaccination against the flu is especially important this season to preserve medical resources and reduce the burden on the healthcare system.
5. There are more doses of flu vaccine available this season than ever before, with 197.4 million doses already distributed.
6. It takes about two weeks for antibodies to develop after receiving the flu vaccine, so it's important to get vaccinated now to be protected for the flu season.
7. Encouraging loved ones and the community to get the flu vaccine can help protect more people.
8. Participating in NIVW activities, such as social media campaigns and webinars, can help raise awareness about the importance of flu vaccination.
9. The flu vaccine can be safely administered during the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.
10. It's not too late to get a flu vaccine, and there's still time to protect yourself and others from the flu.
