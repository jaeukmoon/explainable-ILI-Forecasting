- In Wisconsin, the effectiveness of the 2022-23 influenza vaccine was found to be 54% for preventing medically attended influenza A infection among individuals aged <65 years. 
- The vaccine was also found to be 71% effective for preventing symptomatic influenza A illness among children and adolescents aged <18 years. 
- These findings suggest that the influenza vaccine provides significant protection against circulating influenza A viruses. 
- Influenza vaccination is recommended as long as influenza viruses are circulating. 
- The effectiveness of the influenza vaccine can vary by season, influenza virus subtype, and antigenic match with circulating viruses.
