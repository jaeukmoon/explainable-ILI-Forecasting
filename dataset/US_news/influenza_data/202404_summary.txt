- This season, the most common influenza viruses are type A(H1N1) and type B.
- The JN.1 variant of SARS-CoV-2, the virus that causes COVID-19, is the most widely circulating variant in the United States and globally.
- Influenza activity remains high in the United States, with more people seeking care for flu compared to COVID-19.
- The mix of influenza viruses circulating varies by region, potentially leading to more severe outcomes among hospitalized patients.
- Vaccination can help prevent severe illness from the flu, and the flu and COVID-19 vaccines are well-matched to the current circulating viruses.
