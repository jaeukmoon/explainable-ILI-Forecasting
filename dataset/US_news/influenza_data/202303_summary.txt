- The 2022-23 influenza season in Tennessee had earlier and increased influenza activity.
- Pediatric hospitalization rates were higher during this season compared to past seasons.
- In November 2022, the rate of influenza-associated hospitalizations among children aged <5 years reached 12.6 per 100,000, comparable to peak levels seen in high-severity seasons.
- Circulating influenza viruses were genetically similar to vaccine components.
- Children were twice as likely as adults to receive a positive influenza test result.
- Children were more than twice as likely as adults to become ill if exposed to someone with influenza.
