There is an update on the Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza A(H5N1) Virus for clinicians and healthcare centers. HPAI (H5N1) Virus is widespread among wild birds and causes outbreaks in poultry. There have been three human cases of HPAI A(H5N1) virus infection identified in dairy farm workers in the United States. The risk to the public is low, but individuals with job-related or recreational exposure to infected birds or animals are at greater risk. The COCA Call provides an update on the current outbreak in the United States and offers information for clinicians on testing, antivirals, and infection prevention and control recommendations.
