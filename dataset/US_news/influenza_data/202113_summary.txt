- Live attenuated influenza vaccine (LAIV) can reduce the severity of heterosubtypic influenza virus infection in ferrets.
- LAIV can induce both mucosal and cellular immune responses.
- A single dose of LAIV specific to one influenza subtype provided full protection against a subsequent challenge with a different influenza subtype in ferrets.
- The reduced severity of disease in the challenged ferrets included less weight loss and fever, as well as faster virus clearance.
- Heterosubtypic protection was associated with interferon-gamma+ (IFN-Î³+) T-cell responses, which were cross-reactive to the different influenza subtype, even in vaccinated animals that had never been exposed to that subtype.
- This study highlights the importance of cross-reactive T-cells in the design of a universal influenza vaccine.
