- Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza (HPAI) has been detected in three new Michigan dairy herds.
- The Michigan Department of Agriculture & Rural Development (MDARD) is involved in monitoring and managing the outbreak.
- The affected dairy herds have been placed under quarantine to prevent the spread of the virus.
- HPAI is a serious threat to the poultry industry and can cause severe illness and death in birds.
- MDARD is working to conduct testing and surveillance to identify and control the spread of the virus.
- The outbreak emphasizes the importance of biosecurity measures and vaccination in preventing the spread of avian influenza.
