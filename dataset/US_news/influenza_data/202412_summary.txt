- People with diabetes are at higher risk of developing serious flu complications.
- Annual flu vaccinations are important for people with diabetes to prevent flu and its complications.
- Injectable influenza vaccines (flu shots) are recommended for people with diabetes, while the nasal spray flu vaccine should be avoided.
- Flu vaccination has been associated with reduced hospitalizations among people with diabetes.
- People with diabetes should also be up to date with pneumococcal vaccination to protect against pneumococcal pneumonia and other serious infections.
