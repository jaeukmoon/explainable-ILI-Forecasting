1. The United States has a strong avian influenza surveillance program.
2. Avian influenza viruses circulate freely in wild bird populations without causing sickness.
3. Avian influenza has been detected in both wild birds and commercial/backyard birds.
4. Recent detections include the Eurasian H5 strain of highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) in wild birds.
5. Ongoing monitoring is essential to track avian influenza and prevent its spread.
