1. CDC provides an update on its response activities related to the multistate outbreak of avian influenza A(H5N1) virus, or "H5N1 bird flu," in dairy cows and other animals in the United States.
2. The outbreak of H5N1 bird flu is a current concern and ongoing monitoring is taking place.
3. Public health strategies are being implemented to prevent the spread of bird flu, including guidelines for backyard flock owners.
4. Clinical guidance is available for healthcare providers on the treatment and diagnosis of human infections with avian influenza viruses.
5. Testing and diagnostic protocols are in place for patients suspected of having an infection with novel influenza A viruses.
6. Interim guidance has been provided for the follow-up of close contacts of infected individuals and the use of antiviral medications for chemoprophylaxis.
7. The CDC is actively monitoring the situation and providing updates on the current situation summary of H5N1 bird flu.
8. The CDC also offers information on other related topics, such as seasonal flu, swine flu, flu vaccines, flu forecasting, and the burden of flu.
9. The public is urged to stay informed and take necessary precautions to prevent the spread of avian influenza.
