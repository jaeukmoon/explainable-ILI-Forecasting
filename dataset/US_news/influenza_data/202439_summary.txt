- The 2024 Annual News Conference discussed the importance of protecting people this fall with recommended vaccines against flu, COVID-19, respiratory syncytial virus (RSV), and pneumococcal disease.
- CDC and NFID released reports and data on flu vaccination coverage, flu hospitalizations, and the effectiveness of flu vaccination in preventing hospitalizations during the 2023-2024 flu season.
- During the 2023-2024 flu season, there were between 380,000 and 900,000 flu hospitalizations, and flu vaccination prevented 105,000 to 240,000 hospitalizations.
- COVID-19 vaccination likely prevented an additional 40,000-100,000 hospitalizations during the 2023-2024 typical respiratory virus season.
- Experts emphasized the importance of getting vaccinated against flu, COVID-19, RSV, and pneumococcal disease to protect against respiratory diseases this fall.
- Getting vaccinated is recommended not only for personal protection but also to shape the course of the season and reduce hospitalizations.
- Experts highlighted the role of vaccines in preventing severe illness and reducing the burden on healthcare systems.
