- An outbreak of influenza A(H3N2) occurred on a university campus in Michigan in October and November 2021.
- The outbreak was significant because it was one of the first substantial influenza activities during the COVID-19 pandemic.
- A total of 745 laboratory-confirmed influenza cases were identified during the outbreak.
- More than 95% of the influenza cases were detected between November 1 and 19, indicating rapid spread.
- The majority of cases were among young adults, with a median age of 19 years.
- Genetic analysis of the influenza viruses revealed that they belonged to the A(H3N2) 2a.2 subgroup.
- Approximately 26.6% of individuals with positive influenza test results had received the 2021-2022 influenza vaccine at least 14 days before the test.
- The outbreak highlights the importance of continued influenza vaccination efforts, even during the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Public health measures, such as vaccination and testing, are crucial for controlling and preventing the spread of influenza.
