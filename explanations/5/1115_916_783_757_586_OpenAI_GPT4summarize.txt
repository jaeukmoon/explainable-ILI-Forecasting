The past time-series ILI occurrences over the 5-week period (Week 1, 2021 to Week 5, 2021) show a clear declining trend: 1115 (Week 1, 2021) → 916 (Week 2, 2021) → 783 (Week 3, 2021) → 757 (Week 4, 2021) → 586 (Week 5, 2021). This consistent decrease indicates significantly suppressed influenza-like illness activity during this time, reaching the lowest value of 586 in Week 5, 2021.

2. Despite the declining trend in past ILI occurrences, the future occurrence at 698 (Week 10, 2021) indicates a slight increase from the Week 5, 2021 value of 586. This modest rise suggests a positive correlation between suppressed ILI activity during the observed weeks and a gradual recovery or rebound following the 5-week period. However, this is not a sharp increase, reflecting the suppressed overall influenza activity during this season.

3. Several factors from the summarized reports align with the future ILI increase:
   - Outpatient visits for ILI percentages declined from 1.7% (Week 1, 2021) to 1.1% (Weeks 4 and 5, 2021), remaining well below the national baseline (2.6%). This suppression reflects low healthcare-seeking behaviors, consistent with the low circulation of influenza. However, the prediction of potential increases in flu activity emphasized by the CDC (Weeks 1–5, 2021) could explain the observed modest rebound to 698 (Week 10, 2021).
   - The COVID-19 pandemic greatly influenced reporting and healthcare-seeking behaviors, with alternative care methods complicating data interpretation across Weeks 1–5, 2021. These factors may have contributed to underreported ILI activity in the observed period and a slight rebound when these effects diminished.
   - PIC mortality remained elevated throughout the period (e.g., 17.2% in Week 1, 2021, to 25.0% in Week 5, 2021), driven mainly by COVID-19. Given the overlapping symptoms of ILI and COVID-19, this factor may have masked or delayed recognition of ILI cases, contributing to the observed increase in Week 10, 2021.
   - While influenza positivity rates in respiratory specimens were exceptionally low (e.g., 0.4% in Week 1, 2021, to 0.1% in Weeks 4–5, 2021), CDC cautioned about potential increases in seasonal influenza transmission (Week 1–5, 2021). This expectation aligns with the modest rise in ILI occurrences after 5 weeks.

4. In summary, the future ILI occurrences of 698 (Week 10, 2021) can be attributed to a rebound from the declining trend observed in Weeks 1–5, 2021, alongside low ILI-related outpatient visits, suppressed influenza circulation, and significant disruptions in healthcare-seeking behavior influenced by COVID-19. Persistent PIC mortality and CDC warnings of future increases in flu activity also align with this modest rise.
