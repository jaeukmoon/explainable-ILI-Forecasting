5. Changes in healthcare-seeking behaviors affected data interpretation across all weeks, as noted in multiple CDC summaries. These shifts could have delayed or obscured the detection of cases, resulting in accumulated or unrecognized ILI cases contributing to the sharp rise to 1829 after five weeks.
6. Minimal influenza virus circulation was reported, with positivity rates consistently at 0.1% or below in clinical labs (e.g., Week34 and Week36, 2021). This indicates that other respiratory viruses or non-specific respiratory illnesses likely influenced ILI activity and subsequent increases.

In summary, the reported 1829 future ILI occurrences (Week43, 2021) can be attributed to the contrast between the declining past trends and the persistence of PIC mortality above epidemic thresholds, the influence of non-influenza respiratory viruses, disruptions in surveillance due to pandemic-driven behavior changes, and rising respiratory illness activity beyond influenza during the transition to late 2021.
