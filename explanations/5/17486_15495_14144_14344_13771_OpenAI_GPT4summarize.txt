However, higher ILI activity within specific subgroups (e.g., children aged 0–4 years reaching 6.5%–8.8% across weeks) suggests continued ILI transmission in certain populations.

   - **Geographic and jurisdictional variations**: Most jurisdictions consistently reported minimal activity, with occasional outliers reporting higher levels (e.g., one state with high activity in Week 25). This localized activity may have contributed to sustaining ILI occurrences near the 13,526 level.
   - **Co-circulation of respiratory pathogens**: Continued co-circulation of other respiratory pathogens (e.g., SARS-CoV-2 and RSV as noted in Week 28) likely influenced ILI counts, complicating clear distinctions between influenza and broader respiratory illness trends.
